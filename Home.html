Biography

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro
February 5, 1985
Funchal, Madeira, Portugal

Early Life: Cristiano Ronaldo was born on February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal. From a young age, he showed immense talent and passion for football, joining Andorinha when he was just 7 years old. By age 12, he moved to Lisbon to join Sporting CP's youth academy, a major step in his budding career.

Career Beginnings: At 16, Ronaldo was promoted to Sporting's first team, impressing with his skill and speed. His performance caught the attention of Manchester United's manager, Sir Alex Ferguson, leading to his transfer to the Premier League club in 2003.

Personal Life: Ronaldo is a family man, known for his close relationship with his mother and children. He is also a philanthropist, regularly donating to various charities and causes around the world.

5 UEFA Champions League Titles

Cristiano Ronaldo's dominance in the UEFA Champions League is unparalleled. Over his illustrious career, he has secured the prestigious trophy five times. His first triumph came with Manchester United in 2008, where he played a pivotal role in their victory, scoring in the final and being named the tournament's top scorer. His subsequent four titles were won with Real Madrid in 2014, 2016, 2017, and 2018. During his time at Real Madrid, Ronaldo was instrumental in their success, consistently delivering crucial goals and performances. He holds the record for the most goals scored in a single Champions League season (17 in 2013-14) and is the all-time top scorer in the competition, cementing his legacy as one of the greatest players in Champions League history.

7 League Titles (Premier League, La Liga, Serie A)

Cristiano Ronaldo has proven his exceptional talent across Europe's top leagues, winning seven league titles in three different countries. During his time at Manchester United, he won three Premier League titles (2006-07, 2007-08, 2008-09), showcasing his incredible skill and consistency. After his move to Real Madrid, Ronaldo added two La Liga titles (2011-12, 2016-17) to his collection, becoming a key figure in the club's domestic success. His journey continued in Italy with Juventus, where he won two Serie A titles (2018-19, 2019-20), demonstrating his ability to adapt and excel in different footballing environments. Ronaldo's success in these top leagues highlights his versatility and enduring excellence.

1 European Championship with Portugal (2016)

Cristiano Ronaldo's crowning achievement on the international stage came in 2016 when he captained Portugal to victory in the UEFA European Championship. Despite suffering an injury in the final against France, Ronaldo's leadership and inspiration from the sidelines were crucial as Portugal secured a historic 1-0 win in extra time. Throughout the tournament, Ronaldo's performances were pivotal, scoring three goals and providing key assists. His decisive contributions helped Portugal lift their first major international trophy, and his emotional celebration after the final whistle became an iconic moment in football history. This victory solidified Ronaldo's legacy as a leader and a winner on the biggest stages.

1 Nations League with Portugal (2019)

In 2019, Cristiano Ronaldo once again led Portugal to international glory by winning the inaugural UEFA Nations League. Portugal hosted the final stages of the tournament, and Ronaldo delivered a masterclass performance in the semi-final against Switzerland, scoring a hat-trick to secure a place in the final. In the final against the Netherlands, Portugal emerged victorious with a 1-0 win, and Ronaldo's influence throughout the tournament was undeniable. This triumph added another prestigious trophy to Ronaldo's collection and demonstrated his continued impact and importance to the national team. Winning the Nations League further affirmed Ronaldo's status as a footballing legend and a key figure in Portugal's football history.

Ballon d'Or Awards

Cristiano Ronaldo's remarkable career is adorned with five Ballon d'Or awards, recognizing him as the best player in the world on multiple occasions. These prestigious accolades highlight his exceptional skill, consistency, and impact on the sport.
